Class Projects

Box Blueprint Generator
❮ ❯

The box project was the final project that I had to complete for Dr. Reeve’s Software Engineering 1 class. The goal was to create a console based Java program that generated the svg code for a box based on the inputs of the user. The svg code then would be used by the laser cutters as guidelines to cut out the six sides of the box. We were not able to use any adhesives for the final product, so accurate measurements were required. I was teamed up with Julio Lopez and Miranda Hixon who were wonderful teammates. I was in charge of getting the core logic and coding most of the program.

Software Engineering 1 was the first class to introduce me to the Agile Method. Although I cannot confidently say that the Agile methodology was followed throughout the development, through Dr. Reeves’ guidelines, we were able to practice some aspects of it. Moreover, this was one of my first semester long team projects that required a lot of communication and collaboration. Through this project, I learned how to leverage the individual abilities of each teammate and make the best outcome out of our capabilities. Being able to contribute to a huge portion of the project made me proud of this project and it is still one of the few projects that are showcased on my github main page.

MIKE Interface
❮ ❯

For the final presentation for HCI, I partnered with O'Brien Verdin and Sam Blalock to create a fitness watch interface called Mike. In this project, we selected a mockup that we have already completed and completed a round of usability testing on five users. We identified at least three tasks that the users will do, created a mockup, created a GOMS analysis, performed usability testing on users, and finally updated the user interface based on the results of the testing. Key features that we implemented were the ability to record an activity, gain insightful data about the exercise, and use the map feature to track your run routes. Main feedback that we received from the users during the user testing involved the user interface being too small or cramped.

Leading up to this assignment were multiple mockups that my partner and I had to create. Through these mockups, I was able to not only learn how to use Figma, but also learned the importance of following HCI principles when designing products. Moreover, user testing was a big part of the assignment and it opened my eyes to how different users would use my products. Most assignments including this particular assignment were done in teams. I once again learned how to leverage each other’s abilities to streamline the process from generating the ideas and implementing them through Figma.

Huffman and LZW Encoding
❮ ❯

The Huffman and LZW encoding project was a console based Java program that I worked on for Dr. Reeves in Software Engineering 2. The main goal of this program was to compress files using two different compression methods: Huffman encoding and LZW encoding. Moreover, I implemented an archiving functionality using tar. Before working on the project, I had to learn how Huffman and LZW encoding worked. To achieve this, multiple weeks were spent working with bits and trees. Once the basics were learned, I started to implement the program in Java and was able to create a lossless compression program.

This project was a great opportunity to remind myself that programming is not easy! Even before we had the opportunity to code, we spent weeks learning the foundations of Huffman encoding. This practice set me into the right mindset of putting in the actual effort to figure out how I would approach a program. Moreover, the joy of solving a problem together and collaborating in and out of class was an experience that I would remember for a long time. Through this project not only did I learn to lay the groundwork for a project, but also experienced the joy of solving hard problems together with others.

Reflection

Choosing to study computer science was not a hard decision to make my last year in high school. Having been influenced by my father, who is a software developer, and loving computers growing up, I knew I wanted to study computer science. However, I did not have much knowledge coming into college. AP Computer Science, some java programming in high school, and making small games using Unity were the bare minimum that I had when coming into ACU as a computer science major. I honestly did not know what I was to expect from ACU and the department, but surely I was excited to start my college career.

Sitting in ITC 110, I remember being around a bunch of people who were enthusiastic about games, programming languages, and all the nerdy things that you can imagine. I sat there not knowing what I liked nor what I wanted to do for the projects. However, I had one thing I knew I was capable of doing: studying. I knew that I could sit down and work on a program and make it work whatever the cause was. I knew that I would not be satisfied with a B and strive to get an A. This meant several hours invested in searching Google, looking for the perfect Stackoverflow page, and watching tutorials on Youtube. I was not afraid to do so and succeeded in doing so.

Most classes, major related or not, were not hard to prepare for and fortunately, I was able to complete most of them with good grades (maybe not Intro to Algorithm). I made sure I stayed focused in class, turned in my assignments on time, and invested time on studying for quizzes and exams. Speaking of Intro to Algorithms, it was probably the first class that I actually struggled with and somewhat gave up on receiving a good grade on the class. I could see myself struggling to stay focused in class not understanding what Dr. Arisoa was saying. I struggled to submit quality work for the assignments as well. Things were just a mess my last semester before leaving ACU for a 2 year gap year.

After my two year gap year, returning back to academics was not easy. All the prep work done in Programming 1 and 2 and all the programming classes I took during my Freshman and Sophomore years were nowhere to be found in my brain. Moreover, with the introduction of AI into the world, studying seemed to feel different than the 2 years at ACU before taking the gap years. However, my thought of getting an A still did not change because I firmly believed that grades did not lie and would reflect the amount of effort I put into studies. Another two years are about to pass and now I see myself writing a self reflection for my Capstone class, which will set the end to my academic journey at ACU.

Coming to Abilene Christian University was a big change that I made in my life. I remember myself being disappointed about coming to Abilene rather than attending bigger state schools. I could not understand why my parents wanted me to go to a Christian university. However, as I grew older and experienced more of what the world was like, I began to appreciate the decisions that my parents allowed me to make. Staying in a Christian community was the wisest decision to make during my transition in life.

I have lived my life inside the “Christian bubble” my whole life. Christian school, friends, and family, looking back, I was blessed to have Christ everywhere in my life. Luckily, coming to ACU extended the blessing into my life in a city far away from home. Christian friends, professors, and faculty members, I was once again surrounded by great people and a great environment. Moreover, God has driven me closer to Him by introducing me to several churches around Abilene and I was able to settle at a small Korean church in Abilene.

International Baptist Church is a small Korean church located near S 1st St. With only 30ish members, the community inside the church was small. Sometimes, I thought to myself whether to attend a bigger church in Abilene due to the lack of younger adults at the church and the lack of fellowship opportunities outside of Sunday service. However, God has reinforced me to stay at the church by providing me ways to serve the church and the elderly. Helping out with the technology, food, and maintenance, there were many ways that a young adult like myself could serve the church community.

Lastly, I thank the Lord for being besides me throughout the years in Abilene. 2020 and 2021 were two years that brought a big challenge to my faith journey. Not being able to attend church put me in a situation where I could get lazy in my relationship with God. However, this was when God reassured me through the church community back home. Watching sermons from my home church through the internet was a blessing and still having great Christians around me was another big blessing that God has given me. The ungrateful attitude of coming to a Christian university has changed through the numerous experiences that God has provided me throughout my time in Abilene.
